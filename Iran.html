Atomic Energy Organization of Iran (AEOI) – Introduction

The Atomic Energy Organization of Iran (AEOI) is the governmental authority responsible for the development and regulation of nuclear technology in Iran. Founded in 1974, it operates under the supervision of the President of Iran and plays a central role in implementing Iran's nuclear policy, both for peaceful energy generation and scientific research. Headquartered in Tehran, the AEOI manages uranium enrichment, nuclear fuel production, nuclear power plant operations, and research reactors. It also serves as Iran’s main liaison with the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA), especially regarding inspections, safeguard agreements, and compliance with international nuclear treaties. The AEOI oversees several key nuclear facilities, including the Natanz and Fordow enrichment sites, the Bushehr Nuclear Power Plant, and the Arak Heavy Water Reactor. Over the years, it has been at the center of global attention due to concerns over the potential military dimensions of Iran’s nuclear program. Despite international controversies, the AEOI maintains that its activities are strictly peaceful and in line with international law, emphasizing nuclear energy’s role in medicine, agriculture, and electricity generation.

Features of Atomic energy organization of Iran:

1. Government Authority on Nuclear Energy:
The AEOI is the main body responsible for formulating and implementing Iran’s nuclear policies. Operates under the supervision of the President of Iran.

2. Peaceful Nuclear:
Officially committed to the peaceful use of nuclear energy, including: Electricity generation Medical applications Agricultural research Scientific innovation

3. Facility Oversight:
The AEOI manages and supervises all major nuclear facilities, including: Natanz and Fordow (uranium enrichment) Bushehr Nuclear Power Plant Arak Heavy Water Reactor Research and development centers

4. Nuclear Fuel Cycle Management:
Involved in the entire nuclear fuel cycle: Uranium mining and milling Conversion and enrichment Fuel fabrication Waste management

5. Research and Scientific Development:
Operates multiple research institutes, such as. Nuclear Science and Technology Research Institute (NSTRI) Promotes nuclear technology in medicine (e.g., radiopharmaceuticals) and agriculture.

6. International Relations and IAEA Coordination:
Acts as Iran’s main point of contact with the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA). Handles safeguards, inspections, and technical cooperation.

7. Education and Training:
Provides specialized education, training, and capacity-building in nuclear science for Iranian scientists and engineers.

8. Strategic and Security Role:
While primarily civilian, the AEOI is strategically important, often involved in geopolitical negotiations (e.g., the JCPOA – Iran nuclear deal). Its facilities have been the subject of sanctions, cyberattacks, and international scrutiny.
